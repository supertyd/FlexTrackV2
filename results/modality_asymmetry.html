Which modality is the lifeline? Drop-RGB vs Drop-Auxiliary

Same model (FlexTrack-V2), same masks, two mirror-image sweeps: dropping the auxiliary modality (thermal / event / depth, RGB kept) vs dropping RGB (auxiliary kept). The gap between the two curves is the modality asymmetry — how much more the tracker leans on RGB.

Conclusions

1 · RGB is the lifeline on every benchmark. Dropping RGB costs 2–12× more than dropping the auxiliary modality (success drop, full removal): RGBT234 −11.6 vs −3.3, LasHeR −15.4 vs −5.7, VisEvent −18.9 vs −1.5, DepthTrack −23.8 vs −5.5.

2 · The asymmetry is largest exactly where the auxiliary is weakest. On VisEvent the event stream is near-redundant (−1.5) yet losing RGB is catastrophic (−18.9) — RGB carries essentially all the signal. On LasHeR (thermal genuinely useful, −5.7) the asymmetry narrows.

3 · Still no collapse. Even RGB fully dropped, the model tracks on the auxiliary modality alone (e.g. thermal-only RGBT234 MSR 58.4) — the CMA-trained model degrades, but keeps working, in both directions.

Edge probe (VisEvent, eval-only)

RGB+event 79.7/62.7 · RGB-only 78.0/61.1 · RGB+Canny edge in the event channels 71.2/54.2 (raw pipeline).

Event's gain over RGB-only is small (+1.5 AUC), but it is not plug-in replaceable by classical edges — substituting Canny maps is out-of-distribution and lands below RGB-only. Whatever little the event stream adds, it is not free edge structure; a trained RGB+edge variant would be needed to test the redundancy mechanism further.
