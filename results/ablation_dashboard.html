§2.6–2.7

Mechanisms interact with orthogonality — they are not additive

The most publishable finding (§6–§7). Each line is a mechanism condition measured with and without the orthogonal loss, on DepthTrack_m F-score. If the effects were independent, all lines would be parallel. They are not: removing ortho helps bidirectional fusion (+2.17) but hurts unidirectional A→R (−2.03) — the lines cross.

DepthTrack_m F-score: +ortho → −ortho

Non-parallel / crossing lines = an interaction effect between orthogonal regularization and the fusion/auxiliary mechanism.

§5

Missing-rate degradation — FlexTrack-V2 (R0 = SOTA)

Inference-time auxiliary-modality drop sweep on the final model (V54 weights + tuned thresholds), full test sets, official metrics. The 0% point reproduces the reported SOTA exactly (RGBT234 93.4/69.9 · LasHeR 77.3/61.9 · VisEvent 81.2/64.0 calibrated · DepthTrack F 67.7). Graceful, monotonic decline everywhere — no cliff.

Success metric, % of full-modality performance

§7

Modality asymmetry — drop-RGB vs drop-auxiliary

Mirror-image sweeps on FlexTrack-V2: RGB is the lifeline on every benchmark — dropping it costs 2.7–12.6× more than dropping the auxiliary modality (RGBT234 −11.6 vs −3.3 · LasHeR −15.4 vs −5.7 · VisEvent −18.9 vs −1.5 · DepthTrack −23.8 vs −5.5). The asymmetry is widest exactly where the auxiliary is weakest (VisEvent). Even RGB fully dropped, the model still tracks on the auxiliary alone — graceful in both directions.

Edge probe (VisEvent, eval-only)

RGB+event 79.7/62.7 · RGB-only 78.0/61.1 · RGB+Canny edge in the event channels 71.2/54.2 (raw pipeline).

The event stream's gain over RGB-only is small (+1.5 AUC) but not plug-in replaceable by classical edges — Canny substitution is out-of-distribution and lands below RGB-only. Whatever little event adds, it isn't free edge structure; verifying the redundancy mechanism needs a trained RGB+edge variant.
